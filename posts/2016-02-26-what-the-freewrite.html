What the hype? The Freewrite.

Posted on February 26, 2016

So everyone is going bananas about the Freewrite these day.

But is the hype justified? Personally, I’d say no. One of the first thoughts I had after I saw the Kickstarter Campaign for the then named Hemingwrite was “this is just a clunky Alphasmart Dana”. And the use case is pretty much the same.

So let’s have a quick check how far tech improved in the last 14 years.

The Freewrite has a ePaper-Display, which greatly benefits battery life. But in terms of screen real-estate the Freewrite does not push as far as it should. For proper writing it should have a bigger display, so you can comfortably have some context on the screen with a font of decent size. With a focus on writing prose I’d suggest an octavo-like format (like this).

With the eyes of someone writing scientific papers and blog posts like this, I’m missing Pandoc support (preferably in a WYSIWYG style). LaTeX would be over the top, but with a somewhat accessible *nix under the hood LaTeX rendering could easily be delegated to a server (think of a dedicated ShareLaTeX client).

It also seems hard to avoid the cloud. FTP/WebDAV access to own ressources would be nice, also for having access to ressources you need to have at hand for writing.

Maybe I’m just not in the focus group.
