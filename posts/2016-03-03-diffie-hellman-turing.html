Whitfield Diffie and Martin E. Hellman receive Turing Award

Posted on March 3, 2016

After yesterday’s post about the SSL desaster I am happy to report on something of a lighter mood.

Whitfield Diffie and Martin E. Hellman just received the ACM A. M. Turing Award for their seminal work on cryptography. Without them, most of what you are used to on the internet would not have been possible or at least significantly delayed. Their protocol for key-exchange is still (and will be for many years to come) one of the most important protocols on the internet.

The 2016 award can also be seen as a statement in the current incarnation of the crypto wars, following the article Keys under doormats (full text as PDF).
